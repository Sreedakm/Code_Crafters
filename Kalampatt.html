Kalam Pattu

Kalam Pattu is a traditional ritualistic art form of Kerala that combines intricate floor art (Kalam) with devotional singing (Pattu). It is an integral part of Kerala's cultural and spiritual heritage, often performed in temples, sacred groves (kaavu), and households during specific ceremonies.

Key Aspects of Kalam Pattu

1. Kalam (Floor Art):

A Kalam refers to a ritualistic drawing created on the floor using natural powders made from substances like rice flour, turmeric, charcoal, and green leaves. These drawings are highly intricate and depict deities such as Bhagavathi (Goddess Kali), Ayyappan, or serpents (Nagas), depending on the purpose of the ritual. The Kalam is a temporary and sacred artwork that is meticulously created and eventually erased as part of the ritual.

Pattu (Devotional Singing):

Kalam Pattu involves devotional songs sung in praise of the deity depicted in the Kalam. The songs narrate mythological stories, glorify the deity, and invoke divine blessings. The singing is often accompanied by traditional instruments like the ilathalam, chenda, and kurumkuzhal (a type of wind instrument).

3. Ritual Context:

The ritual is performed to seek blessings for health, prosperity, protection from evil forces, and fertility. It is part of ceremonies like Bhagavathi Seva, Sarpa Pattu (serpent worship), or other specific deities depending on the occasion. The event is presided over by a priest or ritual performer who conducts the prayers and ceremonies.

4. Performance

The performance typically begins with the creation of the Kalam, followed by lighting lamps and offerings like flowers, coconut, and camphor. The devotional singing, often led by experienced singers, starts in a slow tempo and builds intensity as the ritual progresses. The ritual concludes with the Kalam being symbolically erased, signifying the transient nature of life and the fulfillment of the prayer.

Cultural Significance

Kalam Pattu is deeply rooted in Kerala's folk and ritualistic traditions, blending art, music, and spirituality. It serves as a medium to preserve ancient myths, oral traditions, and the connection between humans and nature.
