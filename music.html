Musical Forms of Kerala

Explore the Melodies

Koythu Pattu

Known for its thunderous beats and rhythmic patterns, Chenda Melam is an essential part of temple festivals and celebrations.

Kallam Pattu

Kalam Pattu is a traditional ritualistic art form of Kerala that combines intricate floor art (Kalam) with devotional singing (Pattu).

Nanthuni Pattu

“Nanthuni Pattu” is a traditional song associated with the Nanthuni, a musical instrument native to Kerala, India.

Pulluvan Pattu

Pulluvan Pattu is a ritualistic song sung during temple festivals, with its own distinct rhythm and melodies.

Navooru Pattu

Navooru Pattu is a traditional folk song from Kerala that represents the culture and rituals of the region.

MargamKalli Pattu

Margamkali is a traditional dance-drama form of Kerala, typically performed by women, and is associated with the Syrian Christian community.

Sopana Sangeetham

Sopana Sangeetham is a traditional form of classical music that is performed in the temples of Kerala, especially in the temples dedicated to Lord Vishnu.

Brahmanipattu

Brahmanipattu is a devotional form of music performed by the Brahmin community during temple rituals.

Arabanamutt

Arabanamutt is a folk music genre from Kerala, associated with traditional agricultural practices and rural life.

Vattappattu

Vattappattu is a folk song sung during the festival of Onam, reflecting the celebration of Kerala’s cultural heritage.
